Particpating in ExploraVision Science Contest

ExploraVision is a STEM project competition for students K-12 hosted by Tobisha and NSTA. ExploraVision challenges students to envision and create new technologies ten or more years in the future through collaborative brainstorming and research of current science and technology.

There are six main categories that fit into the description of the project. The first one is present technology, which is an overview of the problem and the present form of the technology used to solve it. The second one is history, which is a description of the technology's history from its inception. The third one is future technology, which is a vision of what the technology would look like in ten years. The fourth one is breakthroughs, which explains discoveries and advancements that need to be made to make the project a reality. The fifth one is the design process, which is the technology's design and how it is better than other designs. The last and sixth one is consequences, which describes the positive and negative consequences of making this device a reality.

I am working with a group of three other people to create a project for the ExploraVision competition. A brief overview of our idea is a disposable sticker that monitors your four main vital signs, heart rate, blood pressure, temperature, and blood oxygen; calls appropriate personnel when your vitals are abnormal and securely stores all of your health data. This device would use Bluetooth technology to communicate with other devices and store data.

Visit exploravision.org to learn more about the ExploraVision contest.
